Dosa

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian thin and crispy pancake made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It’s enjoyed as a breakfast or snack and is loved for its delicate texture and slightly tangy flavor.

Often served with coconut chutney, sambar, or a variety of fillings, dosa comes in many variations like plain, masala dosa, and rava dosa. It’s a healthy and delicious way to start the day or enjoy a light meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional, for fermentation)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately and soak them in water for 4–6 hours.
  2. Add fenugreek seeds to the urad dal while soaking (optional).
  3. Drain the soaked rice and dal, then grind them separately into a smooth batter using water as needed.
  4. Mix the rice and urad dal batter together in a bowl. Add salt to taste.
  5. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8–12 hours (overnight) until it rises and becomes slightly airy.
  6. Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat. Grease lightly with oil or ghee.
  7. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the pan and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin pancake.
  8. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy.
  9. Fold the dosa and serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or your favorite filling.
